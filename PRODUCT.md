# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Polish institutional buyers: school directors, public-procurement (B2G) officers, and general contractors equipping newly built schools and kindergartens. They arrive via tender process or referral. Context: formal, time-pressured, comparison-shopping against several manufacturers. They need credibility fast — normative compliance (PN-EN 1729, E1 atests) and manufacturing evidence close the deal, not aesthetics.

Secondary: architects and facility managers specifying furniture for commercial or medical projects (segment 4 — dedicated projects).

## Product Purpose

Single-page marketing site for BOSTA Sp. z o.o. (Myślenice, est. 1992) — a heavy-duty school, kindergarten, and office furniture manufacturer with full in-house production (CNC, edge banding, metalworking, powder coating). The site's job: establish credibility for the durability + end-to-end manufacturing story and convert visitors into quote requests. Success = inbound quote-request form submissions from institutional buyers.

The brand's real differentiator is anti-vandal engineering and owned production — not colorful classrooms. The site needs to earn trust with people who buy at scale on public procurement contracts.

## Brand Personality

Industrial precision. Institutional credibility. Unshowy durability.

Not warm-and-welcoming, not playful-classroom-color, not SaaS-minimal. More: a mid-century technical manual from a well-run factory — composed, matter-of-fact, proud of what it actually does.

## Anti-references

- Generic colorful school furniture marketing sites (rainbow grids of stacking chairs)
- SaaS landing page templates with cream backgrounds and gradient buttons
- Any site that leads with "modern, ergonomic solutions" without showing the factory
- Editorial-magazine aesthetics (Fraunces italic, broadsheet columns, drop caps) — too decorative for an industrial B2G manufacturer
- Metric-hero templates with big stat grids ("30 years / 2 plants / 10,000 chairs")

## Design Principles

1. **Let the materials be the color.** Warmth comes from beech wood and factory imagery, not from background tints. The UI chrome is neutral — graphite and near-white — so the photos carry the brand.
2. **Credibility through specificity.** Standards numbers (PN-EN 1729-1:2016), process names (lakiernia proszkowa), addresses, KRS, NIP — concrete detail beats generic claims. Institutional buyers distrust vague.
3. **End-to-end is the story.** The manufacturing sequence (CNC → oklejanie → spawalnia → lakiernia) is not a feature list; it's the proof that BOSTA controls quality at every step.
4. **No factory, no trust.** This is an imagery-led brief. Factory floor, macro material detail, and product-in-room photography carry the argument that copy cannot.
5. **Durability over decoration.** Every design decision should feel like it was engineered, not decorated. Clean corners, functional hierarchy, measured pacing.
